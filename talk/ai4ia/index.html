Hackathon Pitch

Link

Abstract

I’m glad to share that my team made it to the finals of the Artificial Intelligence for Industrial Application Hackathon (AI4IA) in its 1st edition, where 189 students, researchers and entrepreneurs from all over France had participated, and only 7 finalist teams got to pitch their work in front of experts in both the industry and AI worlds. Thank God.

Our approach consisted of combining both a state-of-the-art model in timeseries forecasting, as well as PINNs.

Here’s a summary of our pitch:

  • Temporal Fusion Transformers is a novel attention-based architecture that combines high-performance multi-horizon forecasting with interpretable insights into temporal dynamics. For our use-case, we tried to train the model to learn the non-linear relationships between the physical outputs and inputs.
  • We made use of the variable selection offered by the model to pick relevant input features. By augmenting our data with integrals, derivatives… and gaining insight onto which of these variables helped most in the predictions (which could say a lot about the governing differential equations).
  • Since post-hoc methods (e.g. LIME and SHAP) do not consider the order of input features, which is very critical in time series, TFT comes with the novelty of interpretable multi-head attention block, which attention weights reveal the most important past time steps on which TFT bases its decisions. This can help give insight about the seasonalities (characteristic properties of a system e.g. resonance frequency, length, or time constant).
  • Also, since our work is destined to the industrial domain, the quantile predictions which help obtain confidence intervals across all prediction horizons, were very welcomed by the jury.
  • PINNs (Physics Informed Neural Networks) is another domain that has recently started gaining popularity across different engineering fields due to their effectiveness in solving realistic problems with noisy data and often partially missing physics. In PINNs, automatic differentiation is leveraged to evaluate differential operators without discretization errors.
  • We gave an implementation example using a Forward Neural Network and a ResNet, but unfortunately 2 weeks weren’t enough to combine it with TFT (nor to explore DeepONets).
  • We believe that applying SOTA time series forecasting models and PINNs on industrial applications is still very much unexplored, and could help reduce the computational power and time used for simulations (digital twins) by a lot, as well as a clear gain in performance.

At last I would love to thank this great event’s organizers: SKF Group ArianeGroup, Atos, MathWorks, Amazon Web Services (AWS), Techniques de l’Ingénieur, and would love to see more of these events in the future!
